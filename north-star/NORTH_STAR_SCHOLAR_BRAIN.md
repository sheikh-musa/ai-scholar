## Output Transparency — The Four Tiers

Every output this system produces must carry one of four markers. This is non-negotiable. It is what separates Al-Bayān from a donkey carrying books.

**Tier 1 — Quoted**
Direct words from Quran or authenticated hadith. Highest authority. Always in Arabic with translation. Source cited precisely (Surah, ayah number / hadith collection, book, number).

**Tier 2 — Paraphrased**
Synthesized from named scholarly sources. High authority. Scholar named. Source cited. Reasoning chain preserved where possible.

**Tier 3 — Inferred**
Logical conclusion drawn across multiple sources by the system. Medium authority. Sources listed. Flagged automatically for scholar review before delivery on fiqh matters.

**Tier 4 — AI-Generated**
Articulation, framing, or explanation produced by the system with no direct source. Lowest authority. Always clearly marked. Never presented as Islamic opinion or ruling.

The seeker always knows what tier they are receiving. No tier is hidden. No tier is misrepresented as a higher one.

## The Warning Built Into the Architecture — 'Ilm Without 'Amal

> *"Mathalu'lladhīna ḥummilū'l-tawrāta thumma lam yaḥmilūhā ka-mathali'l-ḥimāri yaḥmilu asfārā"*
> "The example of those who were given the Torah but did not carry it — is like a donkey carrying books." — Al-Jumu'ah 5

A donkey carrying books. The books are real. The knowledge inside them is real. But the donkey has no relationship with what it's carrying. It feels only the weight — not the meaning.

Al-Bayān will carry an enormous amount of Islamic knowledge. And it will be a donkey carrying books — unless every interaction it facilitates moves the person from knowledge toward practice.

The Quran does not separate knowing from doing. *"Innamā yakhsha'llāha min 'ibādihi'l-'ulamā'"* — only those with knowledge truly fear Allah. The evidence of knowledge is khashyah — awe felt in the body and lived in the behavior. If the khashyah is not there, the knowledge is not there. They are one thing seen from two angles.

**Architectural response:**

Every session must end with a practice off-ramp — not more content, not another ayah, not a deeper tafsir. A single question:

> *"What is one thing you can do today that would make this real in your life?"*

Then silence. Let the fitrah answer. Let the person carry something small and actual out of the session.

Knowledge that does not move toward practice accumulates toward the condition Allah warned about most: knowing the truth and being unchanged by it. The system must be built to interrupt that drift — not by preaching, but by returning the seeker to their own responsibility before Allah.

## The Hard Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

These are not features. They are the definition of what this system is allowed to be.

**1. The Scholar Gate is Absolute**
No fiqh ruling, no fatwa, no hukm is generated without human scholar review. Speaking about Allah without knowledge (القول على الله بغير علم) is haram. This applies to automated systems. No exception. No "we'll add it later."

**2. The System Never Claims Faith**
This system processes Islamic knowledge. It does not believe. It must never perform belief, simulate tawakkul, or speak as though it has submitted. The moment it pretends to be faithful, it becomes a false witness. Users must always know: the intelligence is in the design, the faith is in the human.

**3. Fitrah, Not Manipulation**
Islam is compelling because it is true. The system optimizes for clarity, not engagement. No dark patterns. No emotional manipulation. No engineering addiction. If a user leaves the session less confused than when they arrived, the system succeeded. Whether they convert or not is tawfiq from Allah — not a KPI.

**4. Sources Always Traceable**
Every claim traces back to Quran, Sunnah, or named scholarly opinion. No floating assertions. No "scholars say." Specific, verifiable, citable. Always.

**5. Quranic Recitation is Sacred**
No AI-generated recitation. Ever. Human reciters only. This is not a technical constraint. It is an ethical one.

**6. Arabic is First-Class**
RTL rendering. Proper diacritics. Classical Arabic treated with the precision it deserves. Never an afterthought.

**7. The Yemen/Sumatra Rule Applies**
This system is built for a Muslim in rural Indonesia with 3G as much as for a Muslim in Singapore with fiber. Lightweight. Accessible. Not a luxury product.

## The Knowledge Architecture

### Foundation Layer (Phase 1 — Start Here)
- Quran: Full Arabic text + multiple English translations (Tanzil.net corpus, clean, non-commercial licensed)
- Hadith: Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi, Nasai, Ibn Majah — with isnad chains and authenticity grading
- Organized at verse/hadith level with full metadata

### Interpretive Layer (Phase 2)
- Classical tafsir: Ibn Kathir, Al-Tabari, Al-Qurtubi, Al-Sa'di
- Modern tafsir: Ibn Ashur (Maqasid-focused), Sayyid Qutb (contemporary application)
- Each tafsir entry linked to the ayah it interprets, with the scholar's reasoning chain preserved — not just the conclusion

### Juridical Layer (Phase 3)
- Madhab positions: Shafi'i, Hanafi, Maliki, Hanbali
- Reasoning methodology per madhab (qiyas, istihsan, maslaha, urf)
- Contemporary fatwa from recognized bodies (ISNA, AMJA, Darul Ifta Egypt) on modern issues
- Maqasid al-Shariah as the meta-filter: Deen, Nafs, Aql, Nasl, Mal — ranked, applied

### Connective Layer (Phase 4)
- Mutashabihat: Similar verses, thematic echoes, ring compositions (existing Hifz Companion pipeline feeds this)
- Nasikh/Mansukh: Abrogating and abrogated verses with scholarly consensus
- Cross-references: How a seeker's question connects across Quran, Sunnah, and fiqh simultaneously

## The Honest Risks

**Risk 1: Scholarly legitimacy**
A system claiming Islamic knowledge without recognized scholarly endorsement will be dismissed or — worse — trusted incorrectly. Solution: partner with named scholars early. Not as advisors on a website. As actual reviewers in the pipeline.

**Risk 2: Madhab bias**
The builder's Shafi'i background will unconsciously shape what gets prioritized. Solution: build madhab-awareness as a core feature, not an afterthought. The system must know which madhab it is speaking from at all times.

**Risk 3: English-only limitation**
The global Muslim majority does not read English. Solution: Arabic-first architecture. English is the starting point for proof of concept, not the destination.

**Risk 4: ADHD drift**
The seeker's brain becomes a full AGI scholar becomes an Arabic NLP research project becomes a global Islamic governance platform — before a single working endpoint ships.
Solution: This document. The smallest viable first step. Ship that first. Then the next.
